# NETBOOK

A **Netbook** is a small, lightweight, and affordable laptop designed primarily for basic computing tasks such as web browsing, email, and document editing. Netbooks are known for their portability and energy efficiency.

---

## 📌 Features
- Compact size (10–12 inch screens)
- Lightweight design for easy travel
- Long battery life compared to traditional laptops
- Affordable price point
- Basic hardware suitable for everyday tasks

---

## ⚙️ Typical Specifications
- Processor: Low‑power CPUs (Intel Atom, AMD C-series)
- RAM: 1–4 GB
- Storage: HDD/SSD (16 GB – 250 GB)
- Operating System: Windows, Linux, or Chrome OS
- Connectivity: Wi‑Fi, USB ports, sometimes Ethernet

---

## 🚀 Use Cases
- Web browsing and social media
- Word processing and spreadsheets
- Online learning and note‑taking
- Lightweight coding or scripting
- Travel‑friendly computing

---

## 📖 Advantages
- Highly portable
- Budget‑friendly
- Energy efficient
- Simple and easy to use

---

## ⚠️ Limitations
- Not suitable for heavy gaming or video editing
- Limited performance for multitasking
- Smaller screen and keyboard may be less comfortable

---

## 🔧 Setup & Usage
1. Charge the netbook fully before first use.
2. Install necessary updates and drivers.
3. Add essential software (browser, office suite, antivirus).
4. Use cloud storage for saving files due to limited local storage.
5. Keep the system lightweight by avoiding unnecessary apps.

---

## 📚 Conclusion
Netbooks are ideal for students, travelers, and users who need a simple, portable computer for everyday tasks. While they lack the power of full laptops, their affordability and convenience make them a practical choice.
